MART MODULAR CANOPY
SYSTEM
The Smart Modular Canopy System is a weather-responsive outdoor shelter designed to enhance usability and comfort in campus spaces. Using automation and modular design principles, the system integrates environmental sensors to detect rain and temperature changes, allowing automatic adjustments without manual intervention. Inspired by biomimicry and cybernetic theory, its origami-based structure ensures flexibility while optimizing space. Evaluated at Cebu Technological University, the system received a high acceptability rating for its features, performance and aesthetics, proving its effectiveness in adapting to environmental conditions. Future refinements will focus on improving speed, noise reduction, and seamless integration to further enhance its functionality.

HOW IT WORKS

The Smart Modular Canopy System is an automated, weather-responsive outdoor structure designed to provide shade and protection. It operates through a combination of sensors, modular components, and an Arduino-controlled mechanism, enabling seamless adjustments based on environmental conditions.

Step 1:

Environmental Detection

-The canopy system includes rain and temperature sensors that continuously monitor weather conditions.

-When rain is detected, the sensor triggers the system to extend the canopy for protection.

-If the temperature exceeds a predefined threshold, the system automatically expands to provide shade from intense heat.

Step 2:

Automated Canopy Adjustment

-The system's Arduino-controlled wiper motor drives the extension and retraction of the canopy.

-The modular spline mechanism, inspired by origami folding techniques, allows the canopy fabric to unfold and retract smoothly.

-A chain drive and gear system facilitate the movement, ensuring a stable and controlled extension of the canopy.

Step 3:

User-Friendly Operation

-The automation eliminates the need for manual control, making it highly convenient for users.

-The control panel allows manual override if needed, enabling adjustments when preferred.

-The system integrates energy-efficient components, ensuring minimal power consumption for sustainable operation.

Step 4:

Maintenance & Serviceability

-The canopy system is designed for easy maintenance, with interchangeable modular parts.

-Troubleshooting guides and clear user manuals ensure seamless repairs and upgrades.

-The use of weather-resistant materials ensures durability and longevity, even in harsh conditions.

Technical
SPECS

Structural Components

Frame Material: 3/16 x 1" Angle Bars & 3/16 x 1 1/2" Flat Bars (for skeletal structure)

Spline Material: 3" PVC Pipes (outer splines for the mechanism)

Spline Mechanism: 3/8 x 10ft. GI Threaded Rods (for extension and retraction)

Fasteners: Stainless Steel 3/8" Hex Nuts, Aluminum Composite Claddings, Allen Bolts, GI Plain Washers

Base Support: 3 1/2" GI Pipe (stem for structure)

Electronic & Control System

Microcontroller: Arduino Uno (controls sensors and motor functions)

Sensors:
Rain Sensor Module (detects precipitation to trigger canopy expansion)
DHT11 Temperature Sensor (monitors heat levels to activate shading)

Motor: Wiper Motor 24VDC (provides motion for spline extension/retraction)

Power Supply: 24VDC regulated output

Control System: Relay Module (controls canopy movement based on sensor input)

User Interface: LCD 2x16 Display (for status monitoring)

Mechanical System

Drive Mechanism: Timing Chain & Gear System (power transmission for canopy movement)

Gearbox: 3D Printed Crown Gear & Spur Gears (drives threaded rod for linear motion)

Spline Covers: 3D-Printed Ball Bearing Housings & Curtain Rod Guides (reduces wear and tear)

Spline Control: Limit Switches (detect canopy extension & retraction positions)

Operational Features

Automation: Automatically extends/retracts based on sensor data

Energy Consumption: Optimized for minimal power use

Weather Adaptability: Reacts to rain and temperature changes

Serviceability: Modular components for easy maintenance and upgrades

Aesthetic Design: Modern visual integration with environmental surroundings
